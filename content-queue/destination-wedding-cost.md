You've said yes. Maybe you're already browsing sunset ceremony photos or pricing flights to the Caribbean. But before you fall in love with a venue, you need a real answer to the question every couple asks first: what does a destination wedding cost?

The honest answer? It depends wildly on where you go, how many people you invite, and whether you book an all-inclusive package or plan everything independently. A [destination wedding in Cancun](/destinations/cancun/) can cost a fraction of a backyard wedding in New Jersey. A clifftop celebration in Santorini can rival the price of a Manhattan ballroom. The numbers only make sense when you break them down by the decisions that actually matter to you.

This guide gives you those numbers, location by location, line item by line item, so you can plan the wedding you've imagined with your eyes wide open.

## What Is a Destination Wedding Cost (and Why It's So Different From a Local Wedding)?

A destination wedding cost is the total you'll spend on your ceremony, reception, travel, and related events at a location away from home. It differs from a local wedding because it bundles travel logistics, currency considerations, and venue-specific packages into one interconnected budget.

According to [The Knot](https://www.theknot.com/content/average-destination-wedding-cost), the average domestic destination wedding runs about $39,000, while international destination weddings average $41,000. Compare that to the $32,000 average for a hometown wedding, and you might assume destination celebrations always cost more. But that headline number hides a crucial detail.

All-inclusive resort packages in Mexico and the Caribbean frequently total between $5,000 and $15,000 for smaller groups, according to [VFL Destination Weddings](https://www.vfldestinationweddings.com/Travel/Destination-Weddings/destination-wedding-costs). That's because the resort bundles your venue, catering, drinks, basic decor, and coordination into one price. You're not hiring a separate florist, caterer, bartender, and venue coordinator. The resort handles it all.

So why does the average still look high? Because plenty of couples choose luxury upgrades, fly in outside vendors, or pick expensive locations. The range is enormous. And that range is exactly why a single "average cost" number is nearly useless without context.

If you're just starting to [plan a destination wedding](/destination-wedding-guide/), the most important thing to understand is this: your final number depends on three big levers you control. Let's look at those next.

## What Factors Affect How Much Your Destination Wedding Will Cost?

Three primary factors shape your total: where you go, how many guests you invite, and what type of venue or package you choose. Everything else flows from those decisions.

**Location** is the single biggest variable. According to [Radical Storage's analysis of global wedding data](https://radicalstorage.com/travel/destination-wedding-statistics/), France averages €30,649 for a destination wedding while Colombia comes in at €4,597. Within the U.S., The Knot reports New Jersey weddings average $54,400 while Utah and Idaho weddings average $17,380. The country, region, and even the specific town you pick will shift your budget by tens of thousands of dollars.

**Guest count** is the second lever. Destination weddings naturally trim your list because not everyone can travel. That smaller headcount directly reduces your spend on food, drinks, rentals, and favors. According to [Paradise Weddings](https://paradiseweddings.com/blog/destination-wedding-statistics/), all-inclusive packages average $6,450 to $6,550 for about 50 guests. Scale up to 80 or more, and you're looking at $22,000 to $30,000 at the same resorts.

**Venue type** is the third lever. An all-inclusive resort bundles most costs into one transparent price. A private villa or independent venue in [Bali](/destinations/bali/) or along the [Amalfi Coast](/destinations/amalfi-coast/) requires you to source and coordinate every vendor separately, which adds both cost and complexity. Flying in a photographer or planner can add $4,000 to $8,000 in vendor travel alone, according to [Eden Photo and Film](https://edenphotoandfilm.com/destination-wedding-cost-breakdown/).

**Season** also plays a meaningful role. Peak months (think December through March in the Caribbean, or June through September in Europe) command premium pricing. Shifting to a shoulder season like late April or early November can save thousands while giving you the same beaches and sunsets with fewer crowds.

## What Does a Realistic Destination Wedding Budget Actually Look Like?

A realistic budget accounts for every category of spending, not just the ceremony and reception. Most couples underestimate by focusing only on the package price.

According to [The Knot](https://www.theknot.com/content/average-destination-wedding-cost), here's how the average destination wedding budget breaks down by category:

- **Reception venue:** $13,030 (the single largest expense)
- **Rehearsal dinner:** $4,184
- **Photographer:** $3,015
- **Wedding planner:** $2,972
- **Alcohol:** $2,844
- **Florist:** $2,833
- **Catering:** approximately $114 per person

A practical framework from [Paradise Weddings](https://paradiseweddings.com/blog/how-to-budget-destination-wedding/) suggests splitting your total budget this way:

- **50%** for your resort stay and wedding package
- **20%** for personal travel and group transfers
- **15%** for group experiences and stay upgrades
- **10%** for extras like a DJ, additional decor, or live music
- **5%** as an emergency fund

That emergency fund matters more than you think. Which brings us to the costs that catch couples off guard.

## Are There Hidden Costs Most Couples Don't See Coming?

Yes, and they can inflate your final total by 10 to 20 percent if you haven't planned for them. Five categories blindside couples most often.

**1. Service charges and gratuities.** According to [Here Comes the Guide](https://www.herecomestheguide.com/wedding-ideas/hidden-wedding-costs), venue service charges typically run 10 to 25 percent of your total. Here's the part that stings: service charges often go to the venue, not the staff. You may still need to tip servers, bartenders, and coordinators separately.

**2. International taxes.** Value Added Tax (VAT) in some countries reaches up to 24 percent, and many venues quote prices as "plus VAT," according to [Kay Northrup Events](https://www.kaynorthrupevents.com/blog-posts/how-to-plan-a-destination-wedding-without-getting-scammed). Budget an extra 5 to 8 percent of each line item for local taxes as a safety cushion.

**3. Vendor travel expenses.** If you're flying in a photographer or videographer, their flights, hotel, meals, and ground transportation become your responsibility. For a [destination wedding photographer](/vendors/photographer/), this can add $2,000 to $4,000 on top of their creative fee.

**4. Currency exchange and transaction fees.** Every international payment chips away at your budget through exchange rate markups and foreign transaction fees. These small percentages add up across dozens of vendor payments.

**5. Legal and documentation fees.** Marriage licenses, translated documents, apostilles, and sometimes blood tests (yes, still required in some Mexican states) can run $200 to $500, according to [Eden Photo and Film](https://edenphotoandfilm.com/hidden-destination-wedding-costs/). Requirements change frequently, so consult your local planner or the country's embassy to confirm current requirements before booking.

A working [wedding planner in Cancun](/vendors/planner/cancun/) or a [wedding planner in Jamaica](/vendors/planner/jamaica/) will know exactly which fees apply and can help you avoid surprises. If you're planning without one, add that 10 to 20 percent buffer to your budget and treat it as non-negotiable.

## Can a Destination Wedding Actually Cost Less Than a Traditional Wedding?

Absolutely, and it happens more often than most people expect. The savings come from smaller guest lists, bundled pricing, and the natural beauty that replaces expensive decor.

According to [Destination Weddings.com](https://blog.destinationweddings.com/average-cost-of-wedding-vs-destination-wedding/), the average destination wedding cost including airfare, accommodations, and the wedding package came to $9,850, compared to $35,000 for the average U.S. wedding. That's a difference of over $25,000.

How is that possible? Three reasons.

First, fewer guests. When your wedding requires a plane ticket, your guest list naturally shrinks. Fewer guests means less food, fewer chairs, smaller cake, and lower bar tabs. According to [Travel Bash](https://www.travel-bash.com/blog/the-real-cost-of-destination-weddings-are-they-less-expensive-than-traditional), guests actually spend slightly less attending a destination wedding ($673 per person) than a traditional one ($703).

Second, bundled packages. All-inclusive resorts like <a href="https://www.sandals.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer sponsored">Sandals</a> include ceremony setup, reception dinner, wedding cake, sparkling wine, and a coordinator in their base packages. You're not paying five separate vendors their individual markups.

Third, natural scenery does the heavy lifting. A turquoise ocean backdrop and a golden-hour sunset replace thousands of dollars in floral installations and lighting rigs. The beach is your decor.

## How to Stretch Your Budget Without Sacrificing the Dream

Smart planning choices can save you thousands without changing the feel of your celebration. Here are the strategies that experienced planners recommend most.

**Choose your destination strategically.** Mexico, the Dominican Republic, and Jamaica offer the best value-to-beauty ratio because of their all-inclusive infrastructure and favorable exchange rates. According to [Paradise Weddings](https://paradiseweddings.com/blog/how-to-budget-destination-wedding/), the average destination wedding in Mexico and the Caribbean costs around $10,500.

**Book during shoulder season.** Late April, early May, and November offer the same beaches and sunsets at lower prices. Resorts are hungrier for bookings, which means better room rates, complimentary upgrades, and more flexible wedding packages. Weekday and Sunday weddings often come with additional discounts.

**Negotiate group room blocks.** Most resorts offer discounted rates, free room upgrades, and sometimes cash-back concessions when you guarantee a block of rooms, according to [DWD Travel](https://dwdtravel.co/how-to-plan-a-luxe-destination-wedding-without-the-price-tag/). Those perks can offset thousands in wedding costs.

**Use what the resort already offers.** On-site vendors, existing furniture, and natural beauty eliminate the need for custom decor and shipping. Ask your resort what's included before spending on extras.

**Trim the guest list intentionally.** Fewer guests doesn't mean less love. It means you can upgrade the experience for the people who matter most: better food, a live musician during cocktail hour, or a private catamaran excursion the day after.

**Combine your wedding and honeymoon.** Staying at the same resort for both eliminates a second round of flights and hotel bookings. Many resorts offer honeymoon suite upgrades as part of their wedding packages.
