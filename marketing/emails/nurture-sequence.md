**Preview text:** Outdoor light is brutal on the wrong settings.

---

Hi [Name,fallback=],

A quick question nobody thinks to ask their jeweler:

How does this ring photograph in direct sunlight?

Most engagement rings are designed for indoor lighting — soft, controlled, flattering. Put them on a beach in Mexico at noon, and the diamond can look flat, overexposed, or just... small in photos.

The rings that actually steal the show in destination wedding photos tend to share a few traits:

- **High-contrast settings** — solitaires and three-stones catch the light and hold it instead of scattering it
- **Elongating cuts** — ovals, elongated cushions, and pear shapes look proportionally larger in wide-angle shots
- **Lower profiles** — thin bands make the center stone the focus, not the metal

## Email 3 — Send: Day 7

**Subject:** Your [Destination,fallback=destination wedding] checklist (save this one)
**Preview text:** 12 months of tasks, in the right order for a [Season,fallback=destination] wedding.

---

Hi [Name,fallback=],

Most destination wedding checklists are built for local weddings with a destination tacked on. The order is wrong. The priorities are wrong. And they completely miss the stuff that actually trips couples up.

Here's what the real timeline looks like:

**12+ months out**
- Choose your destination and narrow to 2–3 resorts or venues
- Check passport expiry dates for you and your guests (many countries require 6 months validity beyond travel dates)
- Start a guest list — not invites, just a headcount estimate. **You mentioned a [GuestCount,fallback=] wedding — if that means 20+ guests traveling, this is when to start thinking about a group hotel block.** Most resorts require an early commitment to unlock group rates, and peak [Season,fallback=season] properties fill up 12–18 months out.

**9–12 months out**
- Secure your venue/resort with a signed contract and deposit
- Book your photographer — good destination photographers fill 12–18 months ahead
- Research the legal requirements for your specific destination (varies enormously — see below)
- If doing a group hotel block: reach out to resort group sales now. This is earlier than most couples think.

**6–9 months out**
- Send save-the-dates. Guests need more lead time for international travel.
- Book your own travel and accommodations separately from guests if you want flexibility
- Get travel insurance. This is the point where you have real deposits on the line.

**3–6 months out**
- Send formal invitations
- Confirm your vendor roster (planner, florist, officiant)
- Handle legal paperwork — apostilles, translations, blood tests (Mexico), notarised documents

**1–3 months out**
- Final guest headcount to resort
- Arrange transportation between airport and resort for guests who want it
- Pre-wedding site visit if you can make it work
