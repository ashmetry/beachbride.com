# BeachBride — Travel Agent / Room Block Revenue Stream

_Last updated: April 2026. All figures from publicly available industry data._

---

## The Thesis

BeachBride has the distribution that every destination wedding travel agency is paying to acquire. The quiz funnel captures couples 12–18 months before their wedding — exactly when room blocks get secured. Converting even 2–3% of email opt-ins to a room block consultation, at $4,000 net per wedding, produces a revenue line that takes years for a standalone travel agency to build from scratch.

**The model:** BeachBride joins a host agency and acts as a travel advisor, securing group room blocks at all-inclusive resorts for destination wedding couples. The service is free to couples — revenue comes entirely from resort-paid commissions of 10–21% per room.

**The traffic-to-revenue comparison:** 50 weddings worth of organic traffic generating affiliate clicks = ~$7,500/year at current rates. Same 50 couples converted to room block clients = $165,000–$275,000. That's a 22–37x lift on the same audience.

**Why Fora over KHM:** The Groups platform, FAM trip access, and technology are better suited to a digital-native operation. **Skip entirely:** InteleTravel (requires joining PlanNet Marketing MLM; 92% of members earned under $41/yr in commissions). Nexion (not suited for new entrants). Outside Agents (adequate but weaker group support than Fora).

|---|---|---|---|---|

**Priority order:** Sandals/Beaches → AMR Collection → Hard Rock → Palace Resorts → Karisma

### Contract types

- **Courtesy/open block:** No fill obligation, group rate guaranteed, minimal perks. Start here.
- **Contracted block:** Agent signs attrition guarantee (80–90% fill). Resort pays commission only on booked rooms; agent/couple liable for unbooked rooms within attrition band. Never sign above 80% attrition until you have 20+ weddings of fill-rate data.

### Certifications (all free)
- **Sandals Specialist:** training.sandals.com — unlocks higher tiers from day one
- **AMR Confidant Learning:** hyattinclusivecollection.com/travel-advisors — unlocks Secrets/Dreams group perks
- **Hard Rock WOW Specialist:** hotel.hardrock.com/travel-advisors — preferred coordinator status
- **Palace Pro Specialist:** palaceproagents.com — base tier for Crown reward access

## Travel Insurance: Upgrade Now

**Switch from InsureMyTrip ($7 flat) to Travel Guard via CJ Affiliate (10–15%).**

| Program | Commission | Cookie | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Travel Guard (CJ)** | 10–15% per sale | 45 days | **Primary — best brand + rate** |
| **Allianz (FlexOffers)** | Up to 12% | 45 days | Secondary — test alongside TG |
| InsureMyTrip (current) | $7 flat | 30 days | Demote to secondary comparison |
| World Nomads | ~$1.50/quote | 60 days | Skip |

A $350 policy at 12% = $42/guest vs. $7. With 20 guests/wedding that's $840 vs. $140 — a 6x lift with zero extra traffic. This switch is independent of the room block business and should happen immediately.

**Group policy upsell:** At the 60-day rooming list mark, pitch the couple a Travel Guard or Allianz group policy covering all confirmed guests — one transaction, higher total premium, one commission. Configure TravelJoy to embed your individual Travel Guard affiliate link in every guest booking confirmation email as the backup.

### Per-wedding task breakdown (steady state)

**Setup — ~2.5 hours total:**
- RFP to 2 resorts (template): 30 min
- Contract review (Fora group support): 45 min
- Build TravelJoy guest booking page: 45 min
- Send couple booking link + info pack (template): 10 min

**Active phase — ~2–5 hours across 12 months:**
- Pickup dashboard check every 2–4 weeks: 15 min each
- Guest questions (biggest variable): 2–6 hours total depending on group size
- Attrition milestone check-ins: 1–2 emails/deadline
- Final rooming list: 30 min (VA compiles, owner submits)
- Post-travel commission follow-up: 30 min if slow

**What the resort handles (not you):** Ceremony, décor, vendor coordination, day-of logistics, on-property guest services.

### VA outsourcing (V2, at 15+ weddings/year)

Offshore VA at ~$10–12/hr, ~10 hrs/week handles: inbound guest questions (scripted responses), payment reminder follow-ups, attrition monitoring, rooming list compilation.

- Cost: ~$6,000/year
- Owner time drops to ~1–2 hours per wedding total
- Owner retains: contract signing (legal), supplier relationships, exception escalations

**V2 economics (25 weddings/year):**
- Revenue: $100,000
- VA: $6,000
- Software: $1,236/yr
- Net: ~$92,764 at roughly 4 owner hours/week

## Cash Flow

No standalone travel commission factoring service exists. Best options:

1. **Charge upfront planning fees** ($500 elopements / $1,000 for 20–40 guests / $1,500 for 40+). Refundable against commission if booking proceeds; non-refundable if couple books independently. This is the primary cash flow bridge.
2. **Fora's bi-monthly cycle** is the standard. KHM pays weekly — revisit if cash flow is a material concern at scale.
3. **Booking.com affiliate links** for guests who book outside the block — smaller amounts, paid monthly, no lag.

## GTM Strategy: The Airbnb/Craigslist Play

### The insight

Airbnb's Craigslist script worked because existing concentrated demand was accessible programmatically, and the offer was better than what the channel natively provided. The equivalent: **wedding planners already have your couples, hate dealing with room blocks, and currently get nothing for it.**

**Direct competitor:** The Room Block Source (theroomblocksource.com) runs this exact model — they pay planners 5% of commission (~$175–350/wedding). Beat them with a flat $400 cash referral. Simpler, higher dollar.

**Legal note:** Planner referral fees must be disclosed to the couple. Disclosed flat fee = standard industry practice. Undisclosed = kickback = illegal. Ask Fora's legal team to confirm your state during onboarding.

### Channel 1: Wedding Planner Outreach (Primary — The Script)

**How it works:** Planners refer couples → you handle everything → planner collects $400 flat per wedding, zero work → couple gets free coordination → you net $1,500–3,500 after referral payout.

**The script:**
1. Run Apify wedding vendor scraper ([apify.com/fortuitous_pirate/wedding-vendor-scraper](https://apify.com/fortuitous_pirate/wedding-vendor-scraper)) — pulls name, email, phone from The Knot, WeddingWire, Zola simultaneously. Budget: ~$75 in Apify credits for 5,000–10,000 planner records.
2. Filter: destination wedding planners, 4+ stars, 10+ reviews, email present.
3. Load into Instantly.ai or Smartlead. 4-touch sequence over 14 days. Lead with their pain (room blocks), not your offer. Subject: *"Do your destination wedding clients ask you to handle hotel blocks?"*
4. Build a one-page partner portal (Notion form initially) — planners submit referrals, track payment status.

**Scale:** At 1% conversion from 10,000 planners = 100 partners. At 2 referrals/partner/year = 200 weddings = **~$700K gross/year at full scale.**

**Passivity: 4/5** | Setup: ~40 hours | Ongoing: ~2 hrs/week

### Channel 2: Free Room Block Calculator Tool (Most Passive)

**What to build:** Interactive calculator at `/tools/room-block-calculator/` — inputs (guest count, destination, % traveling, avg nights) → outputs (rooms needed, cost range by resort tier, booking timeline, resort comparison). Gate full output with email capture. Feeds directly into Stage 1 funnel.

**Distribution leverage:** Give each enrolled planner partner an embeddable iframe for their website. Planner gets a useful client resource; you get the lead. Tool distributes itself.

**Passivity: 5/5** | Setup: 1–2 dev days | Ongoing: zero

### Channel 3: ABC Partnership

The Association of Bridal Consultants (ABC) has a formal room block referral infrastructure for 2,000+ member planners, with relationships at Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, and Caribbean resorts already built. Their planner members are pre-opted into monetizing room block referrals. Getting listed as a preferred resource within ABC puts you in front of the highest-quality segment without cold outreach.

**First action:** Contact ABC directly about preferred vendor or program sponsor.

**Also investigate:** ABC bridal consultant membership reportedly grants 16% Sandals/Beaches commissions without a host agency IATA number. If confirmed, this is a meaningful shortcut for Sandals revenue specifically.

**Passivity: 4/5** | Setup: ~8 hours | Ongoing: ~1 hr/week

### Channel 4: Resort Coordinator Relationships

Every property wedding coordinator signs 20–200 couples/year. After signing, their biggest headache is "where should my 60 guests stay?" You become the answer they forward to every couple. No financial incentive needed — you solve their most annoying post-signing problem.

**Execution:**
1. Complete WOW Specialist (Hard Rock) and Palace Pro Specialist enrollment — both free.
2. Book a FAM trip at Sandals via Fora. Meet the on-site wedding coordinator in person. One relationship with a coordinator doing 50 weddings/year = 50 potential referrals annually.
3. Build a one-page "for wedding coordinators" PDF: what you do, how guests book, your direct booking link.

**Passivity: 3/5** (relationship-heavy) | Setup: ~60 hours | Ongoing: ~3 hrs/week | **Mature: $160–320K/year from 3 property relationships**

## 90-Day Launch Sequence

### Weeks 1–2: Foundation
- Join Fora Travel ($299 annual plan)
- Complete onboarding; prioritize group travel and all-inclusive resort modules
- Get Sandals Specialist, AMR Confidant, and Palace Pro Specialist certifications (all free)
- Sign up for TravelJoy Pro ($32/mo)
- Create Travel Guard CJ Affiliate account; configure affiliate link in TravelJoy confirmation email template (30 min setup, fires automatically per guest forever after)
- Switch insurance CTAs across existing BeachBride content from InsureMyTrip to Travel Guard

### Weeks 3–4: The Script
- Run Apify scraper. Budget $75. Target: 5,000 destination wedding planners, 4+ stars, 10+ reviews.
- Write 4-touch cold email sequence. Touch 1: pain. Touch 2: proof. Touch 3: offer ($400 flat). Touch 4: breakup.
- Launch in Instantly.ai.
- Email BeachBride's existing nurture list about the new room block service — warmest audience, fastest first bookings.

### Weeks 5–8: Build
- Build room block calculator at `/tools/room-block-calculator/`
- Build planner partner submission form (Notion to start; proper portal later)
- Create service page at `/travel-coordination/` — what a room block is, free to couples, Calendly CTA
- Add room block opt-in to Stage 2 quiz

### Months 2–3: Relationships
- Book first FAM trip at Sandals via Fora
- Contact ABC about preferred vendor arrangement
- Goal: 20 enrolled planner partners by day 90; first 2–3 bookings placed

### Month 3+: Compound
- Each planner partner distributes the calculator embed
- Each FAM trip = coordinator relationship = recurring referral flow
- Planning fees arrive immediately; commissions follow 12–18 months later
