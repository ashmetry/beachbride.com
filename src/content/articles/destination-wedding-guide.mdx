Planning a destination wedding is one of the most exciting — and occasionally overwhelming — decisions a couple can make. Done right, it creates an experience that you and your guests will talk about for the rest of your lives. Done without proper preparation, it can become a logistical nightmare.

This guide pulls together everything you need to know: how to choose your location, what things actually cost, how to handle the legal side, and how to find the right team to make it happen.

## What makes a destination wedding different?

A destination wedding moves your ceremony and celebration to a location that's meaningful, beautiful, or simply far from home. The key difference from a local wedding isn't just the geography — it's the *entire experience*.

Destination weddings naturally create a multi-day event. Guests travel together, stay at the same resort, share dinners and excursions. The wedding stops being a single Saturday event and becomes a long weekend or full week. That changes the dynamic completely: less formal, more intimate, more memorable.

The tradeoff is that not everyone can or will attend. The average destination wedding guest list is 30–75 people — compared to 130+ for the average US wedding. That reduction in guest count is both a challenge (you may need to have hard conversations with extended family) and an opportunity (you only celebrate with people who genuinely matter to you).

## How to choose your destination

The best destination wedding location is the one that fits your vision, budget, and guest logistics. Here's how to think through it:

**Legal vs. symbolic ceremony**: Some countries make it easy to legally marry (Mexico, Jamaica, Hawaii). Others make it difficult or impossible for foreigners (Indonesia). Decide early whether a legal ceremony at your destination matters to you, or whether you'd prefer to handle the legal piece at home and hold a symbolic celebration abroad.

**Guest accessibility**: How easy is it for your guests to get there? Cancun and Punta Cana have excellent direct flight connections from most US cities. Bali and Santorini require connections for most North American guests — adding cost and travel time. If you're inviting elderly relatives or families with young children, accessibility matters more.

**Season and weather**: Every destination has a rainy season and a best season. These don't always align with what you'd expect. Santorini's shoulder season (May, September, October) offers better light and fewer crowds than the midsummer peak. Cancun's rainy season runs June–October — not impossible, but riskier for outdoor ceremonies.

**Venue availability**: The world's most iconic venues — the cliffs of Oia in Santorini, the private beaches of Negril in Jamaica — book 12–18 months out. If you've fallen in love with a specific venue, check availability before you fall deeper in love with the location.

## What does a destination wedding cost?

Destination wedding costs vary enormously depending on location, guest count, and the level of service. Here's a realistic breakdown:

| Destination | Budget (30 guests) | Mid-range (50 guests) | Premium (75 guests) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cancun / Punta Cana | $15,000–$25,000 | $25,000–$40,000 | $40,000–$65,000 |
| Jamaica | $12,000–$22,000 | $22,000–$35,000 | $35,000–$55,000 |
| Hawaii | $20,000–$35,000 | $35,000–$55,000 | $55,000–$85,000 |
| Bali | $8,000–$18,000 | $18,000–$30,000 | $30,000–$50,000 |
| Santorini | $25,000–$45,000 | $45,000–$70,000 | $70,000–$110,000 |
| Tulum | $12,000–$25,000 | $25,000–$40,000 | $40,000–$65,000 |

*Estimates include venue, planner, photographer, florals, food and beverage. Excludes guest travel and accommodation.*

## Finding your vendor team

Your vendor team is the most important decision you'll make after the destination itself. At minimum, you need:

**A local wedding planner**: Non-negotiable for destination weddings. A good local planner knows which venues have infrastructure issues, which vendors consistently deliver, which permits take forever, and how to handle the unexpected. Budget $3,000–$8,000 for a full-service planner.

**A photographer**: Your photos and video are the only thing that lasts beyond the week. Don't cut corners here. Invest in someone whose portfolio genuinely moves you. Budget $4,000–$12,000 for photography.

**A venue or resort contact**: If you're using an all-inclusive resort, they typically have an in-house wedding coordinator. Ask specifically about dedicated coordinator vs. shared service — at some resorts, your coordinator is managing 3–4 weddings simultaneously.

## Legal requirements overview

Every destination has different requirements. Here are the key questions to ask:

1. **Can foreigners legally marry?** Most countries say yes, with paperwork. Some (like Indonesia) effectively say no for legal purposes.
2. **What documents are required?** Typically: valid passports, birth certificates (often apostilled/authenticated), proof of single status (if previously married: divorce decree or death certificate), and sometimes blood test results.
3. **How far in advance must documents be filed?** Usually 30–90 days before the ceremony.
4. **Are translated documents required?** Often yes — documents need to be certified translated into the local language.

Your wedding planner handles all of this. It's one of the most important reasons to hire a local expert rather than trying to navigate foreign bureaucracy alone.

## Building your timeline

Working backward from your wedding date:

- **18+ months out**: Book venue and planner, especially for peak-season Santorini, Amalfi, Hawaii
- **12–18 months out**: Book photographer, set your budget, send save-the-dates
- **9–12 months out**: Negotiate room block with your resort, send formal invitations
- **6 months out**: Book remaining vendors (florist, officiant, entertainment), start document collection
- **3 months out**: Submit legal documents, finalize all vendor contracts
- **1–2 months out**: Confirm all vendor details, create wedding day timeline, brief your planner
- **2 weeks out**: Final headcount confirmation, vendor payments, marriage license (if local)

The biggest mistake couples make is underestimating how early venue and planner availability runs out at top destinations. If you're reading this and already 12 months from your target date, start making calls today.
