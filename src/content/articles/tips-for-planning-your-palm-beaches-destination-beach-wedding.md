A Palm Beaches destination wedding gives you something rare: 47 miles of Atlantic coastline paired with ballrooms, museums, and historic estates, all within easy reach of three international airports. Whether you picture toes in the sand or a black-tie reception overlooking the ocean, this stretch of southeast Florida delivers both without forcing you to choose. This [destination wedding guide](/destination-wedding-guide/) breaks down real costs, venue options, the marriage license process, and seasonal timing so you can plan with clarity instead of guesswork.

## Why Are the Palm Beaches Such a Popular Destination Wedding Location?

The Palm Beaches offer a rare mix of beach beauty and upscale indoor venues that few destinations can match. You are not locked into one aesthetic here.

According to [Discover The Palm Beaches](https://www.thepalmbeaches.com/plan/weddings/the-perfect-place-to-start-your-marriage), the region stretches from Jupiter in the north to Boca Raton in the south, giving couples access to 47 miles of sandy coastline plus sophisticated towns with distinct personalities. That means a laid-back surf-town ceremony in Juno Beach and a glamorous reception in Palm Beach proper are both on the table, sometimes on the same day.

A few things set this area apart from other Florida wedding spots like [Key West](/key-west-beach-wedding-destination/):

- **No passport required.** Your guests fly domestic, which removes a major attendance barrier.
- **Venue variety.** According to [Joshua Kane Photography](https://www.joshuakanephotography.com/blog-post/coolest-palm-beach-wedding-venues-a-photographers-guide), Palm Beach County has over 20 unique venue styles, from eclectic warehouses and motorcar showrooms to oceanfront lawns and art museums.
- **Honeymoon built in.** The Florida Keys, Orlando, and the Gulf Coast are all a short drive away, and three nearby airports connect to international flights.
- **Luxury pedigree.** According to [Our Story Imagery](https://www.ourstoryimagery.com/blog/why-palm-beachs-33480-zip-code-is-the-ultimate-destination-for-luxury-weddings/), Palm Beach's 33480 ZIP code is recognized as Florida's top luxury wedding destination.

One common misconception: people assume Palm Beaches weddings are beach-only affairs. The reality is that many couples hold a 15-to-25-minute ceremony on the sand and then move indoors for cocktails and dinner at a nearby resort or gallery.

## Which Palm Beaches Venue Is Right for Your Wedding Style?

The right venue depends on whether you want beachfront simplicity, historic grandeur, or something completely unexpected. Palm Beach County has options for all three.

Here is a quick style matcher to help you narrow things down:

**Beachfront & coastal:** The Seagate Hotel and Spa Beach Club, Eau Palm Beach Resort, and public beach ceremonies from Jupiter to Delray Beach. Best for couples who want ocean views and a relaxed vibe.

**Historic & grand:** The Breakers Palm Beach spans 140 acres of oceanfront property and has been hosting events since 1896, according to [Wedding Style Magazine](https://www.weddingstylemagazine.com/wedding-ideas/wedding-destinations/the-breakers-palm-beach-a-legendary-palm-beach-wedding-venue). The Flagler Museum and Harriet Himmel Theatre also offer old-world elegance.

**Modern & artsy:** The Norton Museum of Art and Lake Pavilion deliver clean lines and contemporary backdrops. According to [Joshua Kane Photography](https://www.joshuakanephotography.com/blog-post/coolest-palm-beach-wedding-venues-a-photographers-guide), options like Elizabeth Ave Station and Grandview Public Market give couples an eclectic warehouse feel.

**Offbeat & unexpected:** Ragtops Motorcars showroom, Museum 66 (a pinball arcade), and the Kelsey Theatre offer story-driven spaces that feel nothing like a standard hotel ballroom.

## How Do You Get a Marriage License in Palm Beach County?

Both applicants must apply in person at a Palm Beach County Clerk of the Circuit Court & Comptroller office with valid photo ID. The standard fee is $86.

According to [Married in Palm Beach](https://marriedinpalmbeach.com/palm-beach-county-marriage-license/), the fee drops to $61 if both applicants are Florida residents and provide proof of completing a state-certified premarital course of at least four hours. Here is the step-by-step process:

1. **Gather your documents.** Both of you need a valid photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport). If either of you was previously married, you will need the exact date and method of dissolution (divorce, annulment, or death of spouse). No copies of divorce decrees are required.
2. **Apply together in person.** You can start the process online, but both applicants must appear at the Clerk's office to finalize. No exceptions.
3. **Pay the fee.** $86 standard, or $61 with premarital course proof.
4. **Wait (maybe).** According to [Florida Court Clerks](https://www.flclerks.com/page/HDI_Marriage_License), Florida residents face a three-day waiting period. This is waived if both applicants are non-residents or if you completed the premarital course.
5. **Use it within 60 days.** The license is valid for 60 days and can be used in any Florida county.
6. **Return it within 10 days.** After the ceremony, the signed license must go back to the Clerk's office within 10 days.

A few things that trip people up: no blood test is required (that is an outdated myth), both applicants must be at least 18, and the courthouse offers a basic ceremony for $30 if you want a simple legal option. Please consult your local planner or the Palm Beach County Clerk's office to confirm current requirements, as these details can change.

## What's the Best Time of Year to Get Married in the Palm Beaches?

Late March through May and late September through November offer the best combination of mild weather, manageable humidity, and golden-hour lighting for ceremonies.

According to [Wedding Bells and Seashells](https://www.weddingbellsandseashells.com/blog/weather-guide-for-florida-beach-weddings/), spring temperatures in the Palm Beaches hover in the mid-to-high 70s with highs in the low 80s. Sunset times stretch from about 6:30 PM in March to 8:00 PM in May, giving you a wide window for late-afternoon ceremonies that catch the best light.

Fall is an underrated pick. According to [Married in Palm Beach](https://marriedinpalmbeach.com/things-every-couple-needs-to-know/), late September through November brings warm breezy days, tapering humidity, and often lower venue pricing.

**The hurricane factor:** According to [Sugar Beach Weddings](https://sugarbeachweddings.com/articles/best-time-of-year-for-a-beach-wedding-in-florida/), hurricane season runs June 1 through November 30, with peak activity from mid-August through late October. This does not make fall impossible, but it does make wedding insurance essential. If you are using our [beach wedding checklist](/beach-wedding-checklist/), add "purchase event insurance" to your to-do list for any date between June and November.

**Summer warning:** June through August brings frequent afternoon thunderstorms, high humidity, and peak vacation crowds. If you must go summer, plan for an early evening start and provide guests with fans, cold water, and shade.

**Winter perk:** December through February offers dry, mild weather and fewer tourists (outside the holidays). Temperatures sit in the comfortable 60s and 70s. The trade-off is earlier sunsets around 5:30 PM, so ceremony timing needs to be tighter.

Also worth knowing: according to [Married in Palm Beach](https://marriedinpalmbeach.com/tips-for-beach-wedding/), turtle nesting season runs March 1 through October 31, which enforces a strict no-lights policy on beaches after dark. Daytime or early evening ceremonies are your best bet during these months.

## How to Build Your Palm Beaches Vendor Team (Without the Guesswork)

Start with vendors who have deep local experience, then fill gaps with specialists who know beach-specific logistics like wind, sand, and ocean noise.

According to [Wezoree](https://wezoree.com/vendors/palm-beach/), over 160 wedding vendors operate in the Palm Beach area, covering everything from florists to DJs. That is a lot of options, which can feel overwhelming. Here is how to approach it:

**Prioritize local over imported.** A photographer who has shot 50 weddings at The Breakers knows exactly where the light falls at 5 PM in April. A florist who works Palm Beach events weekly knows which blooms wilt in the humidity and which hold up. According to [Discover The Palm Beaches](https://www.thepalmbeaches.com/blog/insider-insight-wedding-vendors-palm-beaches), companies like Daniel Events have provided luxury event design in the area for over 22 years.

**Use free resources first.** The [Palm Beaches tourism board](https://www.thepalmbeaches.com/plan/weddings) offers a free wedding services program staffed by specialists who connect couples with vetted venues and vendors. This costs you nothing and gives you a curated starting point.

**Book your big three early.** Venue, photographer, and caterer fill up fastest. Lock these in 9 to 12 months out for peak season dates.

**Rent a wireless sound system.** Ocean waves are louder than you think. According to [Married in Palm Beach](https://marriedinpalmbeach.com/tips-for-beach-wedding/), wireless speakers are essential for beach ceremonies so guests can actually hear your vows.
