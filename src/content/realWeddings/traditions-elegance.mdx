It's not new to our sight whenever a groom cries tears of joy on his and his bride's first look, but every time we encounter one lucky fella, it's like seeing something fresh again and I'm in awe from such a beautiful emotion.  Just like when I first saw Spencer's picture, our groom from today's feature, of him shedding tears as his bride walked towards him to the altar, such a perfectly captured moment.  But moving on to the event itself, Spencer and Christina had a traditional Jewish ceremony after which, their reception was held in an area overlooking the beach and sunset.

And despite the relaxing vibe of the venue, the way they fixed the decors and lighting created such an elegant look.  Congratulations Spencer & Christina!  Photographer: Christina & Spencer, a couple from Tampa Florida, had a beautiful rooftop wedding in the heart of Costa Rica's pacific jungle corridor, Manuel Antonio.

They incorporated both Jewish & Christian traditions, including the traditional ka signing.  The ceremony itself was conducted by both a Minister (the bride's uncle Reverend Krueger, who was flown in from the US) and a Rabbi (Costa Rica's Gonzalo Vega).  The fifty guests enjoyed an outdoor dinner service followed by dancing inside Punto de Vista's grand reception area.
